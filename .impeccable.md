## Design Context

### Users
Malaysian families — parents buying educational toys and gifts for children, teens browsing independently, and local sellers listing unique handmade or curated items. Used primarily on mobile (Shopee-trained behavior: scroll, tap, deal-hunt) but also desktop. Context: casual browsing at home, quick purchase decisions, deal-seeking mindset.

### Brand Personality
Fun, Trusted, Local — like Shopee but for the kids and family niche. Warm and energetic, not clinical. Southeast Asian marketplace energy: vibrant, deal-forward, community-driven. The "Local" dimension means it should feel close to home, not corporate.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light mode. Warm white surfaces, orange-led palette with teal accents.
- **Energy**: Vibrant without being chaotic. Think Shopee's vibrancy dialed down slightly for a family-friendly audience.
- **Reference**: Shopee's deal-focused, badge-heavy, warm energy — but with more visual breathing room and trust signals.
- **Anti-reference**: Cold blue-gray SaaS dashboards, minimal/editorial aesthetics, heavy dark modes.
- **Font direction**: Baloo 2 (display/headings) + Nunito (body) — both warm and rounded, Baloo 2 adds that distinct South/SEA-influenced personality for headings.

### Design Principles
1. **Warmth first** — Every surface, shadow, and border should carry a hint of the orange brand hue. No pure grays.
2. **Deal energy** — Badges, prices, and CTAs should feel urgent and clear. RM prices are heroes.
3. **Trust through clarity** — Clean product cards, seller names prominent, no visual clutter that hides key info.
4. **Mobile-native** — Touch targets generous, cards stack cleanly, navigation is thumb-friendly.
5. **Local personality** — Emoji use in navigation should be minimal/gone; warmth comes from color and type, not decoration.
